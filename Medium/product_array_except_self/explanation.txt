Pattern:
The brute force work because we just look at the left and right of the current index. 
But how can we simplify the process without looking at the left and right side of the current index? 
Well, we separate them. But how does it work? Instead looking at the answer, 
I look at how their indices work. 
Right: [(n/a), (3), (3, 2), (3, 2, 1)] 
Left: [(n/a), (0), (0, 1), (0, 1, 2)] 

If we look at the indices, we can see a pattern for each answer: 
ans: [Right[3] + Left[0], Right[2] + Left[1], Right[1] + Left[2], Right[0] + Left[3]]

Data Structure:
Array

Invariant:
Multiply all numbers except for the current i.

Time Complexity:
O(n)
